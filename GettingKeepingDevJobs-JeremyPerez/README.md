# Getting and keeping a developer job in this market from the perspective of a hiring manager

## Jeremy Perez

### Boise Code Camp 2026 - May 2nd, 2026

The dev job market is a lot tougher than it used to be, and a lot of people are finding that what worked a few years ago just isn’t cutting it anymore. In this talk, I’ll walk through how hiring actually works from the perspective of someone who’s reviewed a ton of resumes, run interviews, and had to make real hiring decisions with limited spots.

We’ll cover why good developers still get passed over, what actually stands out (and what doesn’t), and how to present your experience in a way that makes sense to the people making the hiring decision. I’ll also share some of the small things that tend to quietly knock candidates out of the process.

Then we’ll switch gears to staying employed once you land the job. Expectations are higher, teams are leaner, and it’s not always obvious how to show your value. We’ll talk about how to make your work visible, build trust with your team, and avoid being the person who gets cut when things get tight.

No gimmicks will be offered and participation will be expected.
