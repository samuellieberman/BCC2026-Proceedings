# Robots Don't Need Sprint Boards

## Sean Timm

### Boise Code Camp 2026 - May 2nd, 2026

Most AI initiatives fail to show real value — not because the technology doesn't work, but because organizations are bolting AI onto structures designed for a world of scarce intelligence. Your JIRA board, your sprint ceremonies, your PRD process, your approval chains — these exist because information moved slowly and engineering capacity was expensive. AI changed both of those things. Most organizations responded by giving everyone a chatbot and calling it transformation. The ones pulling ahead rebuilt how decisions get made, how work flows, and how teams are structured.

As someone driving AI strategy at an LMS company — reshaping how engineering and product work together to build software from the ground up — I'll walk through what we're building toward, what we've learned so far, and the questions we're still answering. Yes, I'll show you the tools — but more importantly, I'll show you why the tools don't matter if the organization they plug into wasn't designed for them.
