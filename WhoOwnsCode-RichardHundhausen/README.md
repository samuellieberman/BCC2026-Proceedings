# Who Owns the Code?

## Richard Hundhausen, Bradlee Frazer

### Boise Code Camp 2026 - May 2nd, 2026

In this session, we confront an uncomfortable truth every modern developer is now facing: when AI generates your code, you may not actually own it. Current U.S. copyright law requires human authorship, and cases like Thaler v. Perlmutter make it clear that AI-generated outputs do not qualify. Yet tools like GitHub Copilot, Cursor, and Claud Code routinely produce non-deterministic code with no direct link to a human creator, creating a legal void right in the heart of your codebase. What feels like a productivity boost may instead be a ticking time bomb embedded in every feature, class, and pull request.

Join career software developer Richard Hundhausen and intellectual property and internet law attorney Bradlee R. Frazer as they walk through real examples from IP due diligence where companies discovered too late that AI-generated modules could not be licensed, sold, or assigned during audits, fundraising, and mergers and acquisitions. You will see how contractors have unknowingly delivered code they had no legal right to transfer, how AI can silently pull in copyrighted patterns from unknown sources, and why "nobody cares until you get sued" is the most accurate description of the current landscape. If your team is building software with AI generating even part of the code, this session will show you the risks accumulating under the surface and why pretending they do not exist is no longer an option.
