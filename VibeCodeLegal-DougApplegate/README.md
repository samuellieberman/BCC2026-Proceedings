# How Not to Vibe Code Yourself Into a Lawsuit

## Doug Applegate

### Boise Code Camp 2026 - May 2nd, 2026

Guess What? Your Side Project Is a Company Now.

I built it in a weekend.

Vibe coded a full-stack app.
Auth worked. Uploads worked. Payments worked.
It deployed on the first try.

I felt unstoppable.

Then strangers showed up.

They uploaded things I didn’t expect.
They asked me to delete everything.
They connected third-party accounts.
They complained about personal information being exposed.
They asked who owned what they uploaded.
They argued.

That’s when it hit me:

This wasn’t a side project anymore.
It was a system people used and depended on — and I had just inherited real responsibility.

In this talk, we’ll explore what actually changes the moment real users arrive:
* The illusion of “production-ready” in the AI era
* The hidden surface area of modern web apps — personal data, user content, third-party integrations
* How everyday features (auth, uploads, logs) quietly introduce real obligations — yes, including PII and copyright
* Why “delete my data” is an architectural problem, not just a button
* How small shortcuts turn into operational, security, or compliance headaches
* The guardrails I now add on day one

This isn’t a legal lecture.
It’s not an anti-AI sermon.

It’s a candid field report from building fast — and discovering that once strangers use your software, you’re not just a developer anymore. You’re an operator. If you ship fast, you inherit responsibility just as fast. Let’s talk about how to be ready for it.
